# Presentation Section

## Tessellum: Local-First Knowledge Management & Visualization Platform
**Universidad de Oviedo | Escuela de Ingeniería Informática | Trabajo Fin de Grado**
- **Author**: Jorge Carriles Ruiz
- **Standard Reference**: Aligned with UNE 157801:2014 ("Criterios generales para la elaboración de proyectos de sistemas de información")

---

The purpose of this presentation is to provide an initial framework for the documentation of the Tessellum project, justifying the organization of the material and guiding the reader through the structure that will be followed in the subsequent pages. In a project developed within the field of software engineering, the final result cannot be understood only through the description of the implemented product. It must also be supported by a clear exposition of the ideas, concepts, and criteria that give meaning to the decisions taken throughout the work. For that reason, this opening text serves as a brief but necessary introduction, intended to orient the reader before entering into the more detailed parts of the document.

In line with the academic and technical spirit associated with UNE 157801, this presentation does not aim to discuss the project in depth, nor to anticipate the full content of the report. Its function is instead to establish the context of reading, explain the logic behind the documentary sequence, and make explicit the route through which the reader will progressively approach the project. This introductory role is especially relevant in technical documentation, where clarity of structure is essential to ensure that the different parts of the work are interpreted in a coherent and ordered way.

The development of Tessellum responds to a process in which theoretical understanding and practical implementation are closely linked. As a consequence, the document has been organized so that the reader can first become familiar with the conceptual and methodological foundations that justify the project, and only afterwards move on to the detailed explanation of the work carried out. This progression is not merely formal. It is intended to ensure that the later description of the design, construction, and implementation of the software is read with the appropriate prior context, allowing the technical decisions adopted during the development process to be understood more fully.

Accordingly, the following sections will first introduce the theoretical aspects that frame the work. These include the principles, technologies, and general considerations that are necessary to understand the nature of the problem addressed and the rationale behind the proposed solution. Once this theoretical foundation has been established, the document will focus more directly on the project report itself, where the development process, the main design choices, the implementation work, and the resulting characteristics of Tessellum will be presented in a concrete and structured manner.

This arrangement seeks to guide the reader from the general to the specific, from the conceptual basis of the project to its practical materialization as a software product. In this way, the presentation fulfills a double purpose: on the one hand, it justifies the organization of the document; on the other, it provides the reader with the orientation needed to approach the subsequent sections with a clear understanding of their role within the overall report. The result is a more coherent and accessible reading path, consistent with the objective of documenting the project in a rigorous, academic, and technically well-founded manner.
